New Approaches and Evaluation

To address ongoing challenges in snow leopard conservation in China, four innovative approaches are proposed: implementing long-term incentive programs to engage local residents, strengthening international cooperation efforts, fostering better communication among stakeholders, and initiating creative mini-projects to raise public awareness and promote social engagement. These strategies aim to overcome existing limitations in conservation efforts and enhance effectiveness by encouraging community involvement, facilitating collaboration, and inspiring greater support for snow leopard protection.

Long-term incentive program

Human-snow leopard conflict poses a significant threat to snow leopard populations, with aggressive attitudes from local residents driven by perceived livestock threats. Inspired by successful incentive programs in India and Mongolia, a tailored long-term incentive program is proposed for Tibet, aiming to reduce stocking densities and mitigate conflicts while promoting sustainable development. Drawing from India's experience, the program would engage locals, provide compensation for potential losses, and gradually transition to alternative livelihoods such as handicrafts and tourism. Similarly, Mongolia's efforts involved purchasing local products at fair prices to reduce reliance on livestock and engage communities in conservation. The proposed program for Tibet seeks to limit grazing areas, promote alternative livelihoods, and invest in infrastructure, leveraging Tibet's unique culture and landscape to attract tourists. Short-term monetary compensation may be necessary to incentivize long-term conservation efforts.

International cooperation

International communication and cooperation are crucial for effective snow leopard conservation efforts due to the species' high mobility and habitat spanning international boundaries. To address this, we propose enhancing inter-country communication to standardize transboundary regulations and share advanced knowledge and experiences. This would help combat illegal trading and facilitate research collaboration to improve understanding and protection of snow leopards. While previous attempts at cooperation faced challenges, we advocate for a new regulatory system, such as establishing a Freeland for wildlife with agreed-upon standards, involving non-governmental organizations for supervision. By fostering deeper cooperation, we believe snow leopard conservation efforts will be more effective.

Intercountry communication and cooperation: Governments, NGOs, and indigenous communities

Governments bear primary responsibility for snow leopard conservation, providing legal frameworks and policies, yet the involvement of NGOs and indigenous communities is equally vital. NGOs, with their flexibility and global reach, lead transboundary research and foster cooperation, exemplified by initiatives like the comprehensive snow leopard conservation map. Indigenous communities, deeply influenced by Buddhist principles, hold a special reverence for snow leopards and often act as primary protectors due to logistical challenges faced by governments. Integrating NGOs and indigenous communities into conservation strategies through strengthened communication and cooperation can significantly enhance conservation effectiveness, ensuring a unified approach to safeguarding this iconic species.

Innovative mini project (Visual Arts)

We've developed a digital platform leveraging innovative Metaverse technology to engage society and increase public awareness in snow leopard conservation. This platform recreates the Tibetan highlands' ecosystem, allowing users to explore alongside snow leopards and experience their habitat's biodiversity. By offering an interactive experience that fosters empathy and deeper understanding, our platform transforms wildlife conservation education. Built on Metaverse technology, it integrates virtual, augmented, and physical realities, creating a dynamic environment for real-time interaction and impactful conservation promotion.
